#### Room at the Rust All-Hands

How'd this go? How do we feel?

Hristian: We're getting more serious changes into the language.

Pete: It'd be good to solicit more participation. The fls-contributors team could help more. When there are larger changes, they ripple throughout the doc. How do we feel about getting more folks on board?

Hristian: How would we go about recruiting participants?

Pete: I'm open to ideas. I'll go back to the safety critical rust consortium and talk about this at every meeting. Mention wanting to have credibility with the Project and having contributors working on the FLS.

Pete: Eric, TC, how are things like this donee normally?

TC: All teams struggle with this. The Style team is effectively down to two members. The team maintaining the Reference is Eric and me. We're always interested in more people. At the same time, all the teams that need people need people with staying power, a similar vision, aptitude for the work, etc. People need to be around long enough for us to build comfort with them — it's easier to add people to teams than to remove people from them. All teams struggle with this.

Working in the open is one of our strategies for attracting people.  By making our meeting open and our minutes and conversations on GitHub and Zulip public, we hope to make it easy for others to feel comfortable with us and get involved.

The Content Team may also be able to help here. Putting out stories about the work of the team can help in attracting people who are interested in helping with that work.

And, as Pete mentioned, for something like the FLS, direct outreach to companies benefiting from this is always a good thing to do.

Eric: I see effort put in onboarding — that's always difficult in the Rust Project. Being able to do reviews, have things people can do. Most people coming in aren't there to do what you want to do but what they do. Important to try figure out what's mutually beneficial. Regarding onboarding: a lot of people bounce off. If there's a lot of comments on their work, friction, they tend to bounce off. If you're soliciting people in your space (safety critical group), you may have better luck, but it's always been a challenge.

Pete: I hear all of that. As we get more serious and there are more complex modifications being made to the language, we'll need to do more of this. When the FLS gets upgraded to the 2024 edition, that will be a lot of work. I'll do my best to take this back to the Consortium.
